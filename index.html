About the Symposium

Fostering Interdisciplinary Collaborations in AI, Science & Technology

Artificial Intelligence is transforming the way we teach, learn, discover, and innovate. From advancing scientific research and engineering design to reshaping pedagogy, healthcare, manufacturing, climate science, and institutional operations, AI is becoming a foundational technology across every discipline.

AI4SE 2026, organized by IIT Hyderabad and its Teaching Learning Centre (TLC) during 21-23 July 2026, aims to bring together leading researchers, educators, industry practitioners, innovators, and policymakers to explore the transformative role of AI in science, engineering, and higher education.

"The symposium will feature visionary keynotes, invited talks, panel discussions, and technology demonstrations covering themes such as Generative AI, AI for Scientific Discovery, Quantum Technologies, Materials, Healthcare, Physical AI, Climate Change, and AI-enabled Teaching, Research, and Digital Universities."

Core Action Plan

  • 1 Educational Integration: AI for innovative teaching, clinical research, and institutional administration.
  • 2 Healthcare Acceleration: Deployment alongside diagnostics leaders like Bharat Biotech, LVPEI, and AGI Hospitals.
  • 3 Domain LLMs: Building dedicated and multi-modal Gen AI models for various domains.

Dr. Sangkha Borah

Assistant Professor, IIT Hyderabad

Title: Machine Learning for Quantum Technologies

The rapid scaling of quantum technologies presents unprecedented challenges in characterizing, controlling, and correcting highly complex, noisy systems. Since 2017, machine learning (ML) has emerged as a transformative solution to these bottlenecks. This talk provides a broad overview of how data-driven architectures are accelerating progress across the entire quantum ecosystem, with a focus on quantum control, error correction (QEC), quantum sensing, and quantum state tomography (QST). Alongside a global survey of the field, I will highlight my own recent contributions at the intersection of AI and fundamental physics. Key topics will include deploying deep reinforcement learning to master non-linear quantum dynamics and accelerate motional mode cooling beyond standard sideband limits. Additionally, I will discuss the use of robust ML-based estimators to mitigate continuous measurement noise for real-time QEC, and novel continuous-monitoring methodologies for no-collapse quantum feedback. By bridging computational intelligence with physical constraints, this presentation illustrates how ML is becoming indispensable for the future of robust quantum information processing.

Brief Bio

Dr. Sangkha Borah serves as an Assistant Professor of Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology Hyderabad, where he heads the Quantum Information, Computing, and Control Theory (QuICCT) Research Group. His research bridges artificial intelligence and fundamental physics, with a core focus on Machine Learning for Quantum Technology, Quantum Error Correction, and Quantum Control. His team also investigates the application of machine learning and quantum concepts to neuromorphic computing, photonics, and condensed matter systems. Before joining IIT Hyderabad, Dr. Borah was a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Max Planck Institute for the Science of Light and the University of Erlangen in Germany, operating within the Munich Quantum Valley Initiative to develop advanced machine learning frameworks for Quantum Error Correction. Prior to this, he spent over four years at the Okinawa Institute of Science and Technology (OIST) in Japan across various quantum units. During the emergent phase of the field, he was a key contributor to some of the earliest foundational work applying Reinforcement Learning (RL) to quantum control. Dr. Borah earned both his Ph.D. and Master’s degree in Physics from the Indian Institute of Technology Guwahati.
